# Portfolio Design Plan

## Core Concept
Wisely's portfolio is now a Rulebreaker Lab: a raw but intentional research desk for VDP thought process, AI/security tools, CTF/HTB archive, and activity logs.

## Product Pillars
- Field Notes: VDP thought process, hypotheses, dead ends, turning points, and proof paths.
- Builds: AI tools, automations, experiments, and security-learning utilities.
- Archive: CTF challenges, HTB machines, solve logs, writeup status, search, filters, and activity map.
- Activity Trail: visible proof of movement and iteration.

## Visual Direction
- Mood: rule-breaking, underground lab, blackbox notebook, terminal fragments, case files.
- Layout: asymmetric broken grid, clipped panels, tilted notes, dense metadata strips.
- Texture: thin grid, torn/cut panel edges, small labels, high-contrast typography.
- Palette: black/ink base, toxic green action color, amber warning notes, sparse electric blue.
- Copy: stay lorem ipsum until the real personal wording is ready.

## Page Concepts
- Now: landing lab desk with current obsession, weird idea queue, evidence cards, and links into the core areas.
- Archive: keep the HTB/CTF functionality intact while making it feel like an evidence locker.
- Field Notes: future home for VDP reasoning and case-study thought process.
- Builds: future home for AI tools, automations, and experiments.

## UX Rules
- Keep Archive functionality intact.
- Keep labels meaningful even if body copy is placeholder.
- Preserve keyboard access, focus states, and reduced-motion behavior.
- Let the grid feel broken, but never let text overlap or overflow on mobile.
